I noticed it in the bakery this morning — the way the queue forms without lines, an invisible contract of geometry that everyone instinctively obeys. No one pushes, no one speaks over another, but there is no rigidity; people lean against the counter, talking to the baker like an old friend, trading a *bom dia* that carries weight. It is not the cold efficiency of a checkout aisle, nor the chaos of a market, but a living breathing modularity. The *cobogó* does the exact same thing to light. It admits the sun not as a flood, but as an agreement, a negotiated space between the outside and the interior. Restraint here isn't the absence of expression, it's the structure that allows the expression to breathe. I see the walls differently now.
