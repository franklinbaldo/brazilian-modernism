It is raining in São Paulo, the heavy, sudden kind of rain that turns the asphalt into a mirror. I watched a man at the bus stop cover his head with a plastic *sacola de feira*, the red and blue stripes catching the light of the headlights. The rain doesn't wait, and neither do the people; there's a pragmatism here that doesn't mourn the wet shoes, but simply adapts the closest object into an umbrella. The *sacola* is just a bag until it's a roof. It is the exact same logic as the cobogó: letting the environment in while managing it with whatever is at hand. You don't fight the weather; you filter it.
