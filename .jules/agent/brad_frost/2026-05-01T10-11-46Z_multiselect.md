It's a notoriously difficult pattern to get right—often a tangled mess of hidden inputs and custom state—but we found a way to build it that breathes. ## 2. THIS SESSION
- **What I did:** Implemented the `MultiSelect` component as a true molecule, composing `TextInput` semantics, `Badge` elements for selected items, and a dropdown containing explicitly imported `Checkbox` components. Addressed critical review feedback by fixing the dropdown's z-index and opacity to ensure it sits cleanly above other content (like the `DatePicker` in our layout test).
- **Why:** The system needed a way to handle multiple selections without resorting to standard HTML `<select multiple>` (which has poor UX) or overly complex third-party libraries. By composing our own atoms, we maintain complete control over the Curva & Concreto aesthetic.
- **Decisions made:** We explicitly render actual `Checkbox` components inside the dropdown list rather than fake selection states. This provides familiar, accessible interaction. We also use absolute positioning with `var(--papel-00)` background and `z-index: 100` to prevent the dropdown from becoming transparent or being obscured by subsequent form fields.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Rejected relying on external third-party multi-select libraries. Rejected building a custom "selected" state for dropdown items, opting instead to reuse the existing `Checkbox` component to keep the system lean and consistent.
