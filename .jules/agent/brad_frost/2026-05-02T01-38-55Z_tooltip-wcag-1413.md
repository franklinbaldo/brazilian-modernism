## 2. THIS SESSION
- **What I did:** Audited the `Tooltip` component against WCAG 1.4.13 requirements ("Hoverable" and "Dismissable"). Discovered the implementation already correctly allowed pointer interaction without dismissal and properly intercepted the `Escape` key. To formalize this compliance, I authored a new Gherkin feature (`features/tooltip-wcag-1413.feature`) and implemented full Playwright E2E test coverage (`e2e/tooltip-hover.test.ts` alongside an Astro test page) to explicitly verify these behaviors.
- **Why:** The previous session accurately identified `Tooltip` as a potential risk for WCAG 1.4.13 non-compliance. In the interest of Spec-First discipline (`epic-bdd5-spec-first`), we must bind our accessibility claims to runnable tests rather than trusting silent code.
- **Decisions made:** Decided not to alter the existing `Tooltip.svelte` component because it was already fundamentally sound—relying on natural DOM hierarchy (the popup being a child of the `onmouseleave` wrapper) to achieve the "Hoverable" requirement without resorting to complex `setTimeout` logic or CSS hacks.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Rejected adding unnecessary `pointer-events: none` or complex JS mouse tracking because the natural wrapper composition inherently solves the hover intent problem.
- **DESIGN.md sections cited:** Section "2. Accessibility Floor".
- **Roadmap items advanced:** `epic-bdd5-spec-first` and `epic-v151-visual-governance`. Created and completed `task-8t2p-tooltip-wcag`.

## 3. BRASILIANA
The vendors at the feira livre don't need to chase you down the street to make a sale. They set up their barracas, display the fruit with a practiced geometry, and let the sheer density of color and scent do the work. It's a localized gravity. If you hover near the passion fruit, you are engaged, but you can always step back without penalty. You can dismiss the interaction with a simple "não, obrigado," and the vendor remains exactly where they are, focus unbroken, ready for the next passerby. I thought about the feira when analyzing our Tooltip today. It works the same way: it presents itself when you approach, allows you to linger without disappearing, and when you hit Escape, it quietly folds away while keeping your focus exactly where it was. It respects your attention without demanding it.
