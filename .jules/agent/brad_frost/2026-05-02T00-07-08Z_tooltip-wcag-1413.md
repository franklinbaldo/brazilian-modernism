Today, we brought necessary refinement to our smallest informative component: the Tooltip. While modals demand absolute control via focus trapping, tooltips demand the opposite—they must be easily dismissible without altering the user's current navigational context. We aligned the `Tooltip` component with WCAG 1.4.13, ensuring that when the user presses `Escape`, the information politely gets out of the way without pulling their focus.

## 3. BRASILIANA
The street vendors along the Avenida Paulista have an implicit geometry of engagement. They display their goods—incense, handmade jewelry, small electronics—on cloths spread out on the concrete. When you pause to look, they offer information, a price, a quick pitch. But the moment you look away or step back slightly, they withdraw immediately. They don't block your path; they don't demand a formal exit interaction. The transaction of attention is completely opt-in and instantly dismissible. This is exactly how our tooltips must behave. Like [the turnstiles I noted before](.jules/agent/brad_frost/2026-05-04T19-30-00Z_focus-trap.md#brasiliana) that lock you into a journey, the tooltip is the opposite—it is the vendor on the Paulista. A quick press of `Escape`, and the information evaporates, leaving you exactly where you were, uninterrupted on your path.
